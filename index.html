ABOUT ME

I graduated from UNC-Chapel Hill in May of 2019 with a B.S. in Mathematics and a minor in Computer Science. I also enjoy learning new languages (currently, Haskell and Julia) and experimenting with computer-generated art.

When I'm not working, you can find me at the climbing gym or on some outdoor adventures. Previously, I worked as a routesetter at Progression Climbing and Fitness in Chapel Hill, NC.   • I like learning new programming languages in my free time! Right now, I've been getting back into Haskell and Julia (python syntax at C speeds?!).
  • I'm also quite interested in the theme of compositionality in science. I really enjoyed the work of Libkind et al. (An Algebraic Framework for Structured Epidemic Modeling),
  • which develops a framework for building complex epidemiological models (systems of differential equations) hierarchically from simpler models.
  • The punchline of that paper is that users of this framework can reliably build complex models using a high-level syntax + semantics, and back out the
  • trivially correct system of differential equations that this model represents! No more worrying about sign errors or turning the algebra crank correctly.
  • Not only does it develop the maths behind this, but it also implements this framework in the Julia programming language!
  • I've also been learning about Graph Neural Networks. It turns out that a lot of NN architectures are just Graph NNs (with a message-passing scheme) operating on
  • specific "domain" graphs. For example, the attention mechanism in Transformers can be understood as message-passing on a complete graph of n tokens!
  • This makes GNNs seem more "discovered" than "invented" when compared to other NN architectures.
